One sequence, on site

Most estimating tools stop at a number. Mark runs the whole decision.

Every step below happens on the visit — not back at the office, not the next day. Mark makes the calls a great office manager would make, in order, before it ever offers the customer a time.

1 · The tech tells Mark what the job is — by voice or text

Right on site, the tech speaks or types what the customer needs, and can add photos so Mark can see the work — perfect for scoping wood rot, mold, or water damage. Got a product to assemble, like a pergola or carport kit from Costco? The tech gives Mark the model number and Mark looks it up. No special app — just the tech's phone.

Live

2 · Mark reads it and prices it — three estimates at once

Mark combines the tech's description, the photos, and any product lookup to identify the work and figure out how many techs and how many hours it takes. Then it builds a Good / Better / Best estimate — all three priced options at the same time — against live material costs, so nobody's guessing or underbidding.

Live

3 · It checks the weather

For outdoor and rooftop work, Mark pulls the forecast and avoids booking the crew into rain or unsafe conditions.

Live

4 · It checks material availability

Mark confirms whether the parts are in stock. If they need to be ordered, it schedules the job for after materials arrive — so the crew shows up to a job they can finish.

Live

5 · It pulls the permit — and prices it

Mark knows which work needs a permit in that jurisdiction, pulls the current City fee, and builds it right into the estimate — so the quote reflects the real, permitted cost, not a surprise later.

Live

6 · It sizes the crew

Mark works out how many techs the job needs and across how many days — then only offers times it can actually staff.

Live

7 · It presents financing

On bigger jobs, Mark lays out the financing options so the budget doesn't kill the deal — the customer can say yes to the work they actually want.

Live

8 · It offers three real open times

Mark texts three day-and-time options that reflect true availability. The customer picks the one that works.

Live

9 · The customer signs — and Mark books the crew into ServiceTitan

One choice locks it in. Mark books the right crew directly into ServiceTitan on the spot. The tech converts the signed estimate to an invoice in ServiceTitan and gets to work — no back-office handoff, no waiting.
