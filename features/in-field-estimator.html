It doesn't just price the job — it books it.

Most estimating tools stop at a number. Mark's in-field agent goes all the way to a scheduled job. Your tech photographs the work, Mark builds a priced Good / Better / Best estimate in seconds using live material pricing, and then it does the part that actually fills your calendar: it hands back open appointment times so the job gets booked before the tech leaves the driveway.

How it works in the field

1
Snap the photos. Your tech takes pictures of the job right on site — no special app, just their phone.
2
Mark reads and prices it. Vision AI analyzes the photos, identifies the work, and builds a Good / Better / Best estimate against current material costs.
3
It texts back three times to book. Along with the estimate, Mark texts the tech three open day-and-time options.
4
The customer picks one. The tech talks it through with the customer — or just shows them the text — and the customer chooses the option that works.
5
The tech texts back the choice. One reply locks it in, and the job is on the schedule before he pulls away.

It schedules the whole job, not just one slot

Mark doesn't just drop a single appointment on the calendar. When it books, it accounts for the size of the work:

  • It assigns as many techs as the project actually needs — not just one body for a multi-person job.
  • It schedules across however many days the work takes to complete, instead of forcing it into a single visit.
  • The open times it offers reflect real availability, so what the customer picks is a slot you can actually staff.
Estimate, present options, and book — all on the visit, while the customer is standing right there. No "we'll email you a quote," no back-office bottleneck, and no job that dies waiting to get scheduled.

Why it closes more work

  • Pricing is consistent and current across every tech, so no one's guessing or underbidding.
