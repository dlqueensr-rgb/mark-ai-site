The wrong crew size costs you either way.

Send one tech to a two-person job and the timeline blows up and the customer's unhappy. Send three to a one-person job and you've burned labor you can't bill. Getting crew size right is the difference between a profitable day and a frustrating one — and Mark figures it out up front.

How it works

  • When Mark scopes and estimates a job, it determines how many techs the work actually requires.
  • That feeds straight into booking — Mark schedules the right number of people, not just one default body.
  • For bigger projects, it pairs crew size with the number of days the job needs, so the whole thing is staffed and sequenced correctly.
  • Your day gets planned around reality, so jobs finish on time and labor doesn't get wasted.
Right-sizing the crew before the job hits the schedule protects both your timeline and your labor cost — the two things that quietly make or break a service day.
