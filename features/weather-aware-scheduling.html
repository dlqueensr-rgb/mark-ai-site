Weather cancellations are avoidable.

When outdoor work gets booked without a glance at the forecast, you find out it was a mistake the morning of — a cancelled job, a wasted slot, a customer who has to be rescheduled. Mark factors the weather in before the job ever lands on the calendar.

How it works

  • When Mark schedules outdoor or weather-sensitive work, it takes the forecast into account.
  • It avoids booking exterior jobs on days the weather would likely cancel them.
  • It steers that work toward days it can actually get done — keeping the schedule realistic.
  • Fewer day-of cancellations means fewer wasted truck rolls and fewer customers left waiting.
