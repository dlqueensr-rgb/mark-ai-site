What it does

A scorecard for every call — and the one thing to fix.

Mark Mentor reviews each call after it's handled, grades it against the standards that matter for your brand, and turns it into coaching your managers can act on in seconds.

📊

Seven-dimension scoring

Every call is graded on objection handling, empathy, discovery, pricing framing, brand adherence, holds and dead air, and turn-taking — for one overall score that reflects how the call actually went.

🏆

Team leaderboard

See every rep ranked by average score, with a per-dimension breakdown and the one area to focus on for each person. Strong closers and reps who need help are obvious at a glance.

🔍

Call-by-call breakdown

Open any call to see the coaching tip, what the rep did well, and the exact coachable moments — with the real quotes and a severity flag. No guessing what to bring to the one-on-one.

🔗

Grounded in ServiceTitan

Whether a call booked is taken from the real job in your CRM — not inferred from what was said. The booking number on the scorecard matches the booking number on your board.

🎯

An honest book rate

Wrong numbers, vendors, and out-of-area calls are kept out of the denominator, so the booking rate reflects real opportunities — and a missed bookable lead can't hide as a "not interested."

🏷️

Tuned to your brand

The rubric isn't a generic call-quality score. It's set to your jobs, your pricing conversation, and your membership pitch — so a great call by your standard scores like one.

How it works

From call to coaching — automatically.

1

The call happens

A customer calls and one of your reps answers. The call is recorded in ServiceTitan the same way it already is today — nothing changes about how your team works.

2

Mark scores it

After the call, Mark reviews the conversation against your rubric, scores all seven dimensions, pulls the real booking outcome from ServiceTitan, and writes a coaching tip with the specific moments to work on.

3

Managers coach

Scores roll up into the leaderboard and a recent-calls feed. A manager sees who's strong, who needs help, and the one thing to fix per rep — without listening to a single recording.

The team view

See your whole team at a glance — and exactly where to coach.

The leaderboard ranks every rep by average score and breaks down each dimension, so the gaps are obvious. A rep can book at a great rate and still have a clear thing to work on — and a rep with a decent score but a low book rate tells you something's off before it costs you jobs.

The "focus on" column does the manager's homework: it surfaces each rep's single weakest area, so every one-on-one starts with the one change that moves the number.
